Working with me

My preferred styles of working, aka how to help me do my best work!

Communication style

  • I prefer asynchronous written communication for anything process driven, particularly when providing clarifications or context, so that I am able to refer to it later.
  • I also love face to face communication but this tends to work better for me for looser structured conversations, ideation, and general socialising.
  • Feel free to schedule meetings with me whenever you want/need to chat – I prefer knowing the topics in advance if possible.
  • Slack is the quickest way to get a response from me. I don't mind being contacted at any time but I will likely only respond during week daytimes.
  • I like expectations to be clear and direct - I prefer not needing to read between the lines.

Things I need

  • Time to think things through, researching ideas and gathering feedback before jumping to decision making mode, particularly for anything that affects people and culture
  • When starting something new, I like to have upfront detail and context - visuals alongside a written explanation is helpful.
  • Knowing the purpose behind why we are doing something as this helps me to engage with it.
  • Time to reflect and a couple of brain-breaks during the day to avoid getting overwhelmed by information overload. I have sensory processing sensitivity so taking these moments to decompress and process my thoughts is essential.
  • I find it easier to concentrate on deep work in the late morning/afternoon so I tend to focus on larger projects and deep work during this time.

Things I like

  • Design thinking for process improvements.
  • Coaching techniques for people empowerment.
  • Improving my knowledge by attending webinars / conferences and reading books about culture, wellbeing, and coaching.
  • Active listening and nurturing an environment that is inclusive and psychologically safe.
  • Mental health awareness and open conversations around wellbeing.
  • Getting to know people, what they are passionate about, and sharing things about my life outside of work.

The ways I like to receive feedback

  • I like timely feedback with specific examples and suggestions on how I / it could be better / different.
  • Face to face delivery works best for me but I prefer to be asked whether I am in a good head space to receive feedback beforehand, and some context ahead of time is also useful for me.
  • I see all feedback as a learning tool and would rather have a conversation than things be unspoken.
